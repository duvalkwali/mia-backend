## What MIA Does

Small and medium businesses receive hundreds of customer messages every day across social media platforms — pricing questions, booking requests, complaints, inquiries. Answering them manually takes 2–4 hours a day and often means slow or missed responses.

**MIA sits in between.** When a customer messages a business on WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook, or other platforms, MIA reads the message, figures out what the customer wants, and drafts a reply that matches the business's tone and brand. The owner sees the draft, clicks approve, and it sends — or sets it to auto-send with no action needed.

**The key differentiator:** Every competitor processes these messages through paid cloud AI services (OpenAI/GPT), charging per message. MIA runs its AI on the operator's own server — so the AI cost per message is **$0**, at any volume, across all platforms.

**Who built it:** The founding team is based in **Canada**. The product will launch first in the North American market, then expand to Europe, then to high-volume emerging markets (India, Nigeria, Brazil).

## Strengths

- **Zero AI cost at any scale** — the only product in this space with no per-message AI fee, across all platforms
- **Multi-platform from day one** — a single subscription covers WhatsApp, Instagram, and Facebook; competitors often charge per platform or restrict channels by tier
- **Data stays local** — customer messages never leave the business's server; a genuine advantage in the EU (GDPR), Canada (PIPEDA), and regulated industries (healthcare, legal, finance)
- **Human approval by default** — competitors automate fully; MIA defaults to showing the owner a draft first, which builds trust and prevents AI errors from going public
- **Learns the owner's style** — the AI adapts to each owner's writing voice over time; no competitor offers this
- **No licensing fees** — built entirely on free software; cost structure is infrastructure-only
- **Flexible AI quality** — one setting change switches from local free AI to GPT-4o for businesses that need higher output quality

## Weaknesses & Threats

### Technical
- **Only WhatsApp works today** — Instagram and Facebook Messenger support must still be built. V2 cannot launch without them.
- **Can only serve one business at a time** — a known bug must be fixed before a second customer can safely be added. This is the most urgent task before any launch.
- **Slow reply drafting (7–20 sec)** — cloud-based competitors draft replies in under 2 seconds. Noticeable in busy settings.
- **AI quality below commercial standards** — good for simple replies; struggles with nuanced, emotional, or complex multi-part questions.
- **No safety net if AI crashes** — no backup system, no alerts, no fallback replies.
- **Photos, voice notes, videos ignored** — very common on Instagram and Facebook; a significant gap for V2.
- **No automated tests** — any code change could break live features with no early warning.

### Financial
- **Pre-revenue** — no billing system built, no paying customer, no validated price point. All numbers in this document are estimates.
- **Unknown customer acquisition cost** — no distribution channel exists yet. In North America, CAC for SMB software typically runs $100–500 via paid channels.
- **Cost advantage narrows at low tenant count** — below ~20 managed customers, per-customer infrastructure cost is comparable to cheap cloud AI. Economies of scale are required.
- **Twitter/X API is expensive** — unlike Instagram and Facebook, Twitter charges $100–5,000/month for API access. Adding Twitter as a platform adds a real operating cost.
- **Platform fee risk** — Meta controls WhatsApp pricing and has changed it before. Instagram and Facebook are currently free but could introduce fees.

### Operational
- **One developer holds all knowledge** — no second person can make a production fix, handle an outage, or continue development if the lead developer is unavailable.
- **No monitoring or alerts** — if the platform goes down, no one is automatically notified.
- **Privacy law compliance not implemented** — PIPEDA (Canada), GDPR (EU), and others require data retention limits and deletion rights. Customer messages are currently stored indefinitely with no policy in place.
- **No data backups** — a server failure means permanent loss of all customer data.

### Competitive
- **No mobile app** — North American business owners expect to manage tools from their phones. All major competitors have mobile apps with push notifications. MIA is browser-only.
- **Zero brand recognition** — ManyChat (est. 2016), Hootsuite (est. 2008), Sprout Social (est. 2010). MIA does not yet exist in any customer's awareness.
- **Setup still requires technical knowledge** — competitors are plug-and-play. MIA's self-hosted option requires configuration that most SMB owners cannot do alone.
- **No outbound tools** — competitors let businesses send promotions and campaigns to contact lists. MIA is inbound-only.
- **Meta is a platform and competitor** — Meta owns WhatsApp, Instagram, and Facebook. If Meta builds a native AI reply assistant into any of these platforms, it competes directly using the same channels MIA depends on. This is the most significant long-term risk.
- **Larger incumbents could add local AI** — the technology is freely available; a well-resourced competitor could replicate MIA's cost advantage within weeks.

## Key Questions for the Financial Model

1. **Pricing for North America** — $49, $99, or $199/month? Higher than emerging-market assumptions; changes revenue projections significantly.
2. **Customer acquisition in Canada/US** — paid ads, content marketing, agency partnerships, or direct outreach? CAC range is $100–500 in this market.
3. **How fast can Instagram and Facebook be added?** — V2 cannot launch without at least two platforms. This is a timeline risk.
4. **Twitter/X inclusion** — the API cost ($100–5,000/month) must be factored in if Twitter is part of V2.
5. **Freemium or direct paid?** — a free tier builds trust in a new market but delays revenue. North American SMBs are comfortable with paid trials.
6. **Funding need** — breakeven is achievable under 50 managed customers at $99/month. A small raise ($50K–150K) could accelerate the go-to-market without giving up significant equity.
